Cape Town is the fastest growing economy in South Africa and is home to 60% of the country's start-ups. There is a high prevalence of VC funding and a greater comparative productivity of tech entrepreneurs. On top of it all, Cape Town offers an unbeatable lifestyle.

Kanina Foss

Writer | Researcher | Coder

Kanina spends a lot of time thinking about the future we all want and how to get us there. She is deeply optimistic about human potential and loves engaging with human ingenuity. She believes Africa has profound contributions to make in this regard.

Before co-founding Toybox, Kanina spent four years immersed in the executive management of a leading research-intensive university, the University of the Witwatersrand (Wits). She was involved in designing Wits’ response to rapid technological advances, including engaging industry on how to accelerate innovation and drive digital entrepreneurship, and served on the board of the Tshimologong Digital Innovation Precinct.

Prior to this, Kanina worked for a number of years in promoting the public understanding of science and technology. She cut her teeth as a news reporter and is also an award-winning journalist. She holds a BSc in Computer Science and Information Systems, and a MSc in Global Change.

Arlene Mulder

Dealmaker | Mathematician | Coder

Arlene is passionate about driving the development of the tech industry and growing digital problem-solving capabilities in Africa. She has always loved exploring the power of mathematics and coding to transform the way we face challenges.

After seven years as an investment banker, Arlene left the finance world to start WeThinkCode_ and pursue her dream of democratising and revolutionising education in order to deliver top tech talent. She is currently on the board of Entrepreneurs’ Organisation (EO) Cape Town, attended the International Visitor Leadership Programme in the US in 2017, and is a global ambassador for She Loves Tech.

When she isn’t working with rockstar coders and inventors, she spends her time on a mountain bike, in her trail running shoes, ticking off her travel bucket list, or reading three books at the same time. Arlene holds a MSc Business Mathematics and Informatics.

Simon Dingle

Cryptocurrency | Design Thinking | Communications

Simon is a broadcaster, designer, technologist and entrepreneur, with a focus on fintech. He believes we are doing money wrong and is trying to fix it.

Simon was on the founding teams of 22seven, Luno and Curve, and before that was technology editor at Finweek Magazine, communications consultant to the Shuttleworth Foundation and columnist for several magazines. He is currently building things at Inves Capital and Phantom Design, hosts a weekly technology radio show on 5FM and speaks at events about blockchain and cryptocurrencies, design, business trends, and innovation.

Simon recently authored a book called In Math We Trust which looks at the evolution of trust, explaining the origins of Bitcoin, how cryptocurrency works, and how you can use these networks to take control of your own financial universe.

Sam Beckbessinger

Design thinking | Financial literacy | Behavioural change

Sam is a South African writer, UX designer, product manager, fintech entrepreneur and money dork who has spent most of the past 10 years building tools to help people manage their money better. She is the co-founder of Phantom Design, a fintech company that helps to build personal financial management apps, cryptocurrency exchanges, and new products for the next frontier in global finance.

Passionate about stories, human behaviour and financial inclusion, Sam combines all three in her quest to help the emerging middle class understand how to take charge of their finances, which she details in her bestselling book, How to Manage Your Money Like a Fucking Grownup.

Sam has a BA Honours Degree, studied Strategy Design at the Gordon Institute of Business Science and was a 2014 Mandela Washington Fellow at Yale University, where she got to high-five Barack Obama (true story).

Phathwa Senene

IoT | Electronics | 3D Printing

Phathwa is a self-taught inventor who focusses on creating inventions that have a direct impact on improving people’s lives.

He runs two companies: the first is a 3D printing and electronics company called Taito 3D, a rapid prototyping service which helps to evolve ideas to actionable prototypes that can be tested in the market. The second is an IoT company called SmartCity, which develops IoT solutions to address problems plaguing South African municipalities.

Some of Phathwa’s inventions include an IoT streetlight which measures noise and air pollution levels, a shack fire reporting device which enables residents in informal settlements to report emergencies at the press of a button, and an IoT broom which enables municipal street sweepers to report incidents like illegal dumping and sends GPS coordinates to the relevant municipal department.

Benjamin Rosman

Machine Learning & AI | Robotics | Research

Benjamin is a Principal Researcher in the Mobile Intelligent Autonomous Systems group at the CSIR in South Africa, and a Senior Lecturer in the School of Computer Science and Applied Mathematics at the University of the Witwatersrand, where he runs the Robotics, Autonomous Intelligence and Learning Laboratory.

His research focuses on reinforcement learning and decision making in autonomous systems, in particular how learning can be accelerated through abstracting and generalising knowledge gained from solving related problems. He also works in the area of skill and behaviour learning and transfer for robots.

Benjamin is the Chair of the IEEE South African joint chapter of Control Systems, and Robotics and Automation. He is a founder and organiser of the Deep Learning Indaba, and an invited lecturer at the prestigious African Masters in Machine Intelligence programme in Rwanda. In 2017, he was a recipient of a Google Faculty Research Award in machine learning, and in 2018, he became a Faculty Member for Singularity University, South Africa. Benjamin holds a PhD in Informatics from the University of Edinburgh.

Refiloe Mpakanyane

Communications | Broadcasting | IoT

Refiloe spent 14 years in radio and television as a news anchor, presenter and producer. She also acted in two films: Clint Eastwood's 2010 film, Invictus and Roli Nikiwe's 2012 film, Nothing for Mahala. Her website and YouTube channel, RefilloeLife, focused on addressing the gap in the mainstream media's offering to women who were not thirst trapping, fashion blogging and generally living only for the gram.

After five years of co-hosting Metro FM's highest rated drive show, TouchDown 326, Refiloe left to pursue an exciting investment and work opportunity in China. Upon returning to South Africa, she began focussing on engendering an urgency among South Africans around how we are shaping our collective future, how our current social contracts hobble us, and how future ones could help us to develop.

Refiloe is currently working on a podcast agency that will among other things focus on creating thoughtful and enlivening content for South Africa and beyond.

Ben Nortier

3D Printing | Robotics | IoT

Ben is an electronic and software engineer with a diverse experience set. He studied a BSc and MSc Electronic Engineering with Computer Science, developing a GPS receiver for satellite operations. Thereafter, he worked as a software engineer, building CAD tools for electromagnetic simulation. In 2007, he moved to London where he helped Alstom to modernise their steam turbine design software and worked for Sky on systems with millions of users.

A very early technology adopter, Ben bought and assembled his first 3D printer in 2011 and was recruited by Siemens to be CTO of a 3D printing startup (Ubimake) in Berlin. He bought his first Bitcoin in 2011 and was one of the first Butterfly Labs ASIC miners in 2014. He built one of the first WebGL CAD systems in the world - an open source 3D modelling tool called Shapesmith.

Ben has also built IoT projects for agriculture and thermal simulation tools for architects. Currently, he is fascinated by reinforcement learning in robotics and the work that OpenAI is doing to develop physical robots using simulated learning environments.

Kenny Inggs

Software Architecture | Development Methodologies | Cloud & Shared Infrastructure

When Kenny was still learning to spell, his dad brought home an 8088 IBM PC. It asked for his name, he typed it in and to his amazement, it said "Hello Kenny". He's been playing with computers ever since.

Kenny has built software internationally and locally for giants like Microsoft and startups like 20twenty. He has been involved with fintech for more than 20 years, from architecting core-banking systems in the late 90's, to building consumer cryptocurrency tech-startups today. He is a co-founder of 22seven and leads software architecture and development at Inves Capital. He is particularly passionate about creating spaces for creative technologists to build high quality systems at full tilt.

Kenny loves technology. He loves technology that can change lives even more. But he loves engaging, accessible life-changing technology most of all.

Matthew Proxenos

3D Printing | Robotics | IoT

Matthew is a mechanical engineer, biomedical engineer and all-round enthusiast about the world. He enjoys thinking about optimizing processes and about the James Webb Telescope.

Matthew is a very technical, analytical thinker. His fields of interest (recently) are in data science and machine learning, but he has been involved in surgical device development (including clinical trials), wheelchair design, and high-end drone manufacture. He likes to get his hands dirty, dislikes monotonous routine, and constantly tries to mix things up.

In terms of hobbies, there is a lot of overlap with his career. He enjoys building little robots and making things like star-trackers for astrophotography. Like any good tinkerer, he has a whole cupboard filled with projects that are 95% complete and gadgets that were cannibalized for parts.
